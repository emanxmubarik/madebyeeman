About Me

I’m Eman, a journalism student at CCNY with a background in computer science. Through my journalism coursework, I have been able to write stories on a wide range of topics, from homeless shelters to international students. Alongside my reporting, I have experience in coding, from building websites to working on developing a web-scraper.

I am passionate about public service and advocacy. Over time, I have become increasingly interested in investigative and data journalism, especially using data as a tool to highlight systemic issues. In the future, I hope to pursue a career in law focused on international law and human rights, where I can combine storytelling, ethical technology and advocacy to drive change.

In my free time, I enjoy reading books, thrifting and watching the latest movies with my friends. I'm currently reading Babel by R.F. Kuang and looking forward to the release of "Backrooms."

Let's connect if you're interested in collaboration, whether that's to investigate community issues or create platforms to advocate for others!
